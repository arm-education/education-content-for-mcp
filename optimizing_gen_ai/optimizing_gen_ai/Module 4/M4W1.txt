Welcome to this module on Optimization
for CPU Inference.

In earlier modules,
we explored how CPUs can be a 

cost-effective and energy-efficient choice for
running Generative AI.

Now we'll shift our focus to the
practical side—

how to squeeze the most performance out
of your CPU for inference.

We'll begin with algorithmic
optimizations, 

like quantization and pruning.

These techniques reduce the size and
complexity of your models,

making them faster and easier to run on
resource-constrained hardware.

Next,
you'll learn how to optimize matrix

multiplication—the computational core of most AI models—

using techniques like cache aware tiling,
loop reordering, unrolling,

and multi-threading.

From there,
we'll explore how to leverage Arm CPU

SIMD technologies, including NEON, SVE,
I8MM, and SME.

These specialized hardware instructions
dramatically accelerate inference,

especially when combined with low-precision formats.

Finally,
we'll show you how all these methods come

together in KleidiAI,
an open-source library of micro-kernels

designed to run fast,
efficient inference on Arm CPUs.

By the end of this session,
you'll be equipped with both the

techniques and the tools to build
optimized, 

high-performance AI applications.
