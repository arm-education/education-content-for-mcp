Welcome to this module on Machine
Learning Frameworks 

and Optimized Libraries.

In today's AI landscape,
achieving high computational performance

is essential—and CPUs play an essential role in

delivering efficient machine learning
solutions.

CPUs are everywhere,
from laptops to smartphones to data centers.

And with the right tools and techniques,

they can deliver fast,
efficient inference without the extra

cost or energy draw of more parallel
systems.

In this session,
we'll explore how CPUs can be optimized

for Generative AI inference.

You'll begin by looking at the cost and
energy advantages of using CPUs—

especially when deploying at scale or on
the edge.

Then,
we'll examine the growing challenges introduced

by increasingly complex models,
and how graph-based computation helps us

manage and optimize their execution.

From there,
you'll learn about different execution modes—

static graphs and eager mode—and how each one effects 

performance and flexibility.

Finally,
we'll dive into the growing ecosystem of

tools and frameworks like PyTorch,
llama.cpp, and KleidiAI,

all designed to get the most out of your
CPU-based deployments.

By the end of this module,
you'll understand how and why CPUs remain

a smart,
scalable choice for running modern AI models.
