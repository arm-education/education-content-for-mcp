Welcome to this module on optimizing
Generative AI on ARM.

Over the past few years,
Generative AI has exploded—

from massive language models answering questions,

to real-time translation tools running on our phones.

But as these models become larger and
more powerful,

they also demand more from the hardware
they run on.

Arm-based processors are everywhere,
and optimizing Generative AI to run

efficiently on them is a critical
challenge.

In this module,
we'll explore how to make that happen.

You'll begin by looking at how Generative
AI models are growing in size

and why that matters.

Next,
you'll dive into the differences between

CPUs and GPUs and how their architectures
shape performance.

Then,
we'll explore how memory and compute

bottlenecks affect AI inference.

Finally,
you'll learn several techniques

that engineers use to optimize these models,
including quantization, pruning,

and distillation.

By the end,
you'll be better equipped to understand

what it really takes to run Generative AI
on Arm devices

efficiently and effectively.
